Female Serial Killers

Introduction

Female serial killers have never gotten the spotlight that the male ones have. Different motives and a completely different style often make them much harder to catch, giving them higher potential lethality. They aren’t prolific, but they are rare cases that garner special attention nonetheless. We aim to highlight the key differences between male and female serial killers, as well as go in-depth on some of the best examples of “she can do it better than him!”

The Differences and Simularities

by Elaine Music

Usually, when women kill, they are far less brutal in the act than their male counterparts. For they most part, they kill by either poison in most cases, or suffocation. Approximately three quarters of their victims are weak or frail in some way, such as the elderly, the infirm, and small children. On average, one out of every six (less than 15%) serial killers is a woman and kills about six people each. Their motive is most often financial greed. Almost all of them killed people they knew.
More often than not, when women select victims, it is from a familiar environment such as their home (family members) or their place of employment, such as in the cases of killer care-givers.

But Why?
While financial greed is the most obvious motive, studies of female serial killers in the United States, who's crimes occured between 1821 and 2008, revealed other factors. Nearly forty percent of the killers had some form of mental illness. All had experienced high rates of trauma from all forms of abuse. About twenty-five percent had issues with drug or alcohol addiction.
